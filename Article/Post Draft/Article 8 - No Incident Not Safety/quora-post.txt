"Zero Incidents This Quarter" Might Be the Most Dangerous Sentence in Operations

Your data center has zero incidents. That might mean your systems work well. Or it might mean you have stopped detecting drift.

Most facilities cannot tell the difference.

70% of data center outages had detectable precursors weeks or months before they hit (Uptime Institute 2023). The cooling trend that slowly degraded. The battery string with rising internal resistance. The BMS sensor that drifted 2% per month for a year. All visible in the data — if anyone was looking.

Green dashboards mask systemic drift. The absence of failure is not the presence of safety.

This is where Safety-II thinking becomes critical. Safety is not the absence of bad things — it is the presence of positive capacities: detection capability, response readiness, organizational learning, adaptive capacity.

A facility can have zero incidents and still be drifting toward a catastrophic failure. The precursors are there. The question is whether anyone has the tools, time, and mandate to look for them.
