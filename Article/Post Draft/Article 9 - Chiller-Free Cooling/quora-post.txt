Jensen Huang Said "No Chillers Needed." HVAC Stocks Lost $1.7 Billion in Hours. Here Is What He Actually Meant.

At CES 2026, Nvidia's CEO declared that the new Rubin GPU architecture with direct-to-chip liquid cooling eliminates the need for traditional chillers. The market reacted instantly — HVAC stocks dropped $1.7B.

But "no chillers" only tells half the story.

Direct liquid cooling handles chip heat — the hottest component. But a server is not just a chip. Power supplies, memory, VRMs, NVMe drives, fans, and network switches all generate heat. Facility-level loads — lighting, UPS losses, PDU losses — still need cooling too.

The actual breakdown in a high-density AI rack:
- GPU/CPU (handled by DLC): ~65-70% of total heat
- Everything else (still needs air/chiller): ~30-35%

In Nordic climates where ambient stays below 20C most of the year? Yes, you can probably eliminate chillers entirely. Free cooling handles the residual.

In Singapore, Jakarta, or Dubai where ambient hits 35-40C? You still need 40-50% chiller backup for the non-DLC thermal load.

- What happens during a coolant leak?
- What is the thermal runway timeline?

The answer is not binary. It is regional, seasonal, and workload-dependent.
