QUORA ANSWER DRAFT
==================
Target Question: "How do you improve maintenance compliance rates in facilities?"
(Also fits: "What is a good PM completion rate?", "Why do maintenance schedules always slip?", "How to optimize CMMS for data centers?")

---

ANSWER:

Most facilities claim 85-90% PM compliance. When I audit their CMMS data, the real number is usually 55-70%.

The gap? Overdue PMs marked "complete" without execution. Emergency work eating scheduled time. Technicians doing the work but not closing the ticket. PMs scoped so broadly they take 3x the allocated time.

Maintenance compliance is not a discipline problem. It's a workflow engineering problem.

After optimizing maintenance programs across multiple critical facilities, here's what actually moves the needle from 70% to 97%+:

1. Right-size your PM library. Most facilities have 30-40% redundant or obsolete PMs. A generator doesn't need weekly oil sampling AND weekly visual inspection AND weekly run test as three separate work orders. Consolidate.

2. Match resource allocation to workload. If your team has 800 PM hours/month of scheduled work and 640 available labor hours, compliance will never exceed 80%. Math doesn't care about motivation.

3. Fix the CMMS workflow. If closing a work order takes 12 clicks and 3 dropdown menus, technicians batch-close at end of shift. If it takes 2 taps on a mobile device, they close in real-time.

4. Implement a frozen zone. PMs scheduled for the next 7 days cannot be rescheduled without manager approval. This prevents the "I'll do it next week" drift that kills compliance.
