Ever wonder where all those data centers are actually being built?

The global data center industry now has 122 gigawatts of installed capacity. To put that in perspective, that is more than the total electricity generation capacity of most countries. And 12.5 gigawatts more is currently under construction.

The biggest surprise is not the total number but where growth is happening. Northern Virginia still dominates with over 4,000 MW operational and another 5,500 MW planned. But the fastest growing markets are places like Dubai (25% annual growth), Kuala Lumpur (22%), and Nairobi (25%). The traditional European hubs like Amsterdam and Dublin are growing at single digits.

Some wild facts from the data:

Singapore has the tightest market anywhere — less than 1% vacancy and colocation costs nearly $400 per kW per month. Compare that to Mumbai at $125 per kW per month with plenty of available space.

Hyperscalers like AWS, Google, and Microsoft spent $413 billion on infrastructure in 2025 alone. That is an 84% increase from the year before. There are now 1,689 hyperscale data center facilities worldwide.

Power cost differences create huge competitive advantages. Dallas pays $0.055 per kilowatt hour. Singapore pays $0.18. That 3x difference adds up to millions over the life of a facility.
