QUORA ANSWER DRAFT
==================
Target Question: "Is there a data center bubble in Southeast Asia?"
(Also fits: "Is Johor building too many data centers?", "Will Southeast Asia data center market crash?", "Are there too many data centers being built?")

---

ANSWER:

6,068 MW of data center capacity is currently under development in Southeast Asia. For context, the entire existing capacity of the region is roughly 2,500 MW.

That means the pipeline is 2.4x the installed base. In Johor, Malaysia alone, the pipeline is 5,800 MW — for a state that currently has less than 200 MW of data center load.

Is it a bubble? I built a quantitative risk framework to answer this systematically rather than with opinions.

The concerning signals:
- Land prices in Johor's DC corridor have increased 400-800% in 2 years
- Power grid capacity in Johor is ~8 GW total. 5.8 GW of DC demand would consume 72% of state capacity
- Most projects are speculative — pre-leasing rates are below 20% for facilities beyond 2027
- Fiber connectivity into Johor is still catching up. Subsea cables take 2-3 years to deploy

The stabilizing signals:
- Hyperscaler demand is genuinely explosive (AWS, Google, Microsoft all expanding in SEA)
- Sovereign AI mandates (Indonesia's Government Regulation 71/2019, Malaysia's national AI strategy) require in-country data processing
- SEA digital economy growing 15-20% annually

My assessment: 40-50% of the announced pipeline will not be built as planned. Projects without secured power, fiber, and anchor tenants will stall or consolidate. But the surviving projects will serve very real demand.
