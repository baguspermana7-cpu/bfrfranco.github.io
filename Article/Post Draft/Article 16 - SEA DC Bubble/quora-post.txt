6,068 MW of Data Centers Planned in Southeast Asia. Is This a Bubble?

Johor, Malaysia alone has a 5.8 GW pipeline. The state has 3.8 million people. That is 12x the current operational capacity.

At 20% annual absorption — aggressive by any historical standard — it takes until 2035 to fill the pipeline. And that assumes zero additional supply enters the market.

I wrote the bear case. Not because I believe the industry will crash, but because every investor and operator needs to stress-test their assumptions.

The uncomfortable questions:

1. Hyperscaler dependency: 60-70% of new capacity is pre-leased to 4-5 companies. Those same companies are self-building. Every MW they self-build is one they will not lease.

2. Announced ≠ deployed: AWS's $6B? Over 15 years. Microsoft's $2.2B? Over 4 years. These are gradual deployments, not the lump-sum injections that pipeline math assumes.

3. The bullwhip effect: 2024 announcements trigger 2025 builds that deliver 2027-2028. If AI efficiency improves faster than demand grows, new supply hits a weakening market.

4. Legacy stranding: Existing DCs at 5-10 kW/rack cannot serve AI workloads at 50-100 kW/rack. They face dual compression — losing traditional tenants to cloud while unable to attract new AI tenants.
