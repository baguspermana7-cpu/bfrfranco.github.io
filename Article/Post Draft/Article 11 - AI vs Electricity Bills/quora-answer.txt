QUORA ANSWER DRAFT
==================
Target Question: "Do AI data centers increase electricity prices for regular consumers?"
(Also fits: "How much electricity does an AI data center use?", "Are data centers causing power shortages?", "Will AI raise my electricity bill?")

---

ANSWER:

One AI data center consumes as much electricity as 100,000 households. That's not an exaggeration — it's basic math.

A 100 MW AI campus running at 85% utilization = 744 GWh/year. Average household consumption in Southeast Asia = 3,000-7,000 kWh/year. Do the division.

But does that actually raise YOUR electricity bill? The answer is more nuanced than headlines suggest.

It depends on three things:

1. Grid capacity margin. If the grid has surplus generation capacity, adding a data center doesn't increase prices — it increases utilization of existing assets, which can actually lower per-unit costs for everyone. If the grid is already constrained, the data center competes for the same electrons, and prices rise.

2. Tariff structure. Most countries charge industrial consumers different rates than residential. In Malaysia, large industrial users pay MYR 0.337/kWh vs residential at MYR 0.218/kWh. Data centers cross-subsidize residential users in this model.

3. Infrastructure investment. Here's the counterintuitive part: data centers often trigger grid upgrades that benefit everyone. A 200 MW data center campus requires a new substation and transmission lines. That infrastructure serves the surrounding area too.

But the risk is real in grid-constrained markets. In Singapore, data centers consume ~7% of total electricity. In Ireland, it's 18% and rising. When data centers grow faster than generation capacity, something has to give — and it's usually industrial electricity prices first, then residential.
