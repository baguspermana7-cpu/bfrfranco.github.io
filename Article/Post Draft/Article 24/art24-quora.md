# Quora Post

Target Questions:
1. What are the best-paying jobs that don't require a college degree?
2. Is data center technician a good career?
3. How do I become a data center engineer?
4. What jobs are created by AI data centers?
5. Is there a shortage of data center workers?

---

## Answer

I can speak to this directly — I have been working in data center operations for over 12 years, starting as an industrial electrical engineer and working my way to Engineering Operations Manager.

The short answer: data center operations is one of the best career paths in technology right now, and demand is accelerating because of the AI boom.

Here are the numbers. HVAC engineer demand in data centers is up 67 percent since 2022. Robotic technician demand is up 107 percent. The United States needs 300,000 new electricians just for AI data center construction, according to BlackRock and the IBEW. Meanwhile, 65 percent of data center operators report they cannot find enough qualified candidates.

The salaries are strong and do not require a four-year degree:
- Entry-level DC technician: $45,000-$57,000
- Mid-level (3-5 years): $68,000-$84,000
- Senior (6-10 years): $105,000-$142,000
- Liquid cooling specialist: $90,000-$160,000
- DC engineer: $92,000-$141,000
- AI infrastructure specialist: $140,000-$200,000

Data center technician compensation has jumped 43 percent in the past three years due to AI-driven demand. Workers in DC construction earn 25 to 30 percent more than equivalent roles in other industries.

The workforce crisis makes this especially urgent. The average data center engineer is approximately 60 years old. Thirty-two percent of the workforce is over 60, and only 16 percent is under 30. The industry loses 23,000 experienced workers to retirement annually while trying to fill 340,000 projected vacancies.

To get started, the most common entry paths are:
1. BICSI Installer 1 and OSHA 10 certifications (achievable in 6-12 months)
2. Microsoft Datacenter Academy (hands-on training at operational facilities)
3. Google's 18-month workforce development apprenticeship
4. Amazon Career Choice IT Infrastructure Specialist program
5. Community college programs like NOVA's AAS in Data Center Operations

BlackRock recently committed $100 million to train 50,000 skilled trades workers specifically for infrastructure roles including data centers.

A realistic timeline: 6-12 months of training, a $45,000 starting role, and progression to $100,000 or more within 3-5 years if you specialize in high-demand areas like liquid cooling or GPU cluster management.
