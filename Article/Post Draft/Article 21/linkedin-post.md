Something unprecedented happened in data center energy in 2024-2025. Five of the world's largest technology companies simultaneously turned to nuclear power as the answer to AI's insatiable electricity demands.

Not wind. Not solar. Not batteries. Nuclear.

As a data center engineer with 12+ years in critical infrastructure, I have spent the past month analyzing every major deal, comparing SMR technologies, and assessing whether this bet can actually deliver. Here is what I found.

THE DEALS
- Microsoft signed a $1.6B, 20-year PPA to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 (835 MW). Target: 2027. DOE issued a $1B loan.
- Amazon locked in a 1,920 MW nuclear PPA through 2042 with Talen Energy's Susquehanna plant, plus a 320-960 MW SMR fleet with X-energy.
- Google partnered with Kairos Power for 500 MW of advanced reactors by 2035, including the first Gen IV reactor PPA signed by a US utility (TVA).
- Meta announced 6.6 GW across three providers (Vistra, TerraPower, Oklo) -- the largest nuclear commitment in tech history. Powers the Prometheus AI Supercluster.
- Oracle's CEO announced three SMRs for a 1+ GW data center campus.

Combined: over 11.8 GW -- equivalent to 12 traditional nuclear plants.

WHY NUCLEAR
AI training runs 24/7 for weeks. Nuclear: >90% capacity factor vs solar ~25%, wind ~35%. A single AI cluster can consume 1 GW. The IEA projects DC electricity doubling to 945 TWh by 2030. US grid queue: 2,600 GW waiting, 5+ year delays. PJM projects a 6 GW shortfall by 2027.

Co-located nuclear bypasses the queue. In December 2025, FERC ruled data centers can plug directly into nuclear generators.

THE REALITY
No SMR has ever powered a data center. NuScale's flagship: $3.6B to $9.3B before cancellation. Vogtle: 7 years late, $21B over budget.

But Big Tech has $300B+ combined cash, multi-decade PPAs, and a calculation that lost AI revenue from power constraints exceeds any cost overrun.

MY TIMELINE
- 2027: Microsoft TMI restart (existing reactor, high confidence)
- 2029-2030: Canada BWRX-300 (first Western SMR, under construction)
- 2030: TerraPower Natrium (broke ground June 2024)
- 2030-2035: Commercial SMR fleet deployment

SMRs are a 2030s solution being planned today. For 2028 power needs, natural gas and existing nuclear PPAs remain the only options.
