Here's an AI infrastructure story that most people haven't heard the full version of.

Last year, Elon Musk's xAI built the world's largest AI supercomputer in just 122 days. They took an abandoned factory in Memphis, Tennessee, and turned it into a facility running 100,000 GPUs. For context, a project like this normally takes 18 to 24 months. They did it in four.

The scale is hard to wrap your head around. The cluster now runs 230,000 GPUs, consumes 150 megawatts of electricity (enough to power a small city), and uses 168 Tesla Megapack batteries just to buffer the power supply. The target is 2 gigawatts total and 1 million GPUs.

But there's a part of this story that got a lot less coverage.

To power the facility before the grid was ready, xAI installed dozens of methane gas turbines capable of generating 495 megawatts. Without a single Clean Air Act permit. In a neighborhood that already has 22 of Tennessee's 30 largest polluters and the highest childhood asthma hospitalization rate in the state.

The NAACP filed intent to sue. Harvard researchers estimated $44 million per year in health damages from the emissions. The EPA updated their Clean Air Act rules partly in response.

As someone who has spent 12 years building and operating data centers, I genuinely respect the engineering speed. But environmental compliance is not optional overhead you skip to hit a deadline. It exists to protect people who didn't choose to live next to your facility.
