QUORA ANSWER DRAFT
==================
Target Question: "How much water do AI data centers use?"
(Also fits: "Are data centers causing water shortages?", "What is WUE in data centers?", "Can data centers operate without water for cooling?")

---

ANSWER:

A single large AI data center consumes 3-5 million liters of water per day. To put that in perspective, that's enough drinking water for 30,000-50,000 people.

And here's the uncomfortable fact: 58% of the world's data centers are located in water-stressed regions.

Why so much water? Evaporative cooling. Most data centers use cooling towers that evaporate water to reject heat. It's the most energy-efficient method — but it's water-intensive. A 50 MW facility with cooling towers uses roughly 1.8 liters per kWh of IT load.

WUE (Water Usage Effectiveness) is the metric: liters consumed per kWh of IT energy. Industry average is 1.8 L/kWh. Best-in-class dry-cooled facilities achieve 0.1-0.2 L/kWh. But dry cooling uses 15-30% more electricity.

This is the trade-off nobody wants to talk about: save water or save energy. In water-stressed regions like Singapore, parts of India, and the US Southwest, the answer should be obvious. But energy costs are measured in dollars, and water costs are measured in... not much (water is dramatically underpriced globally).

Southeast Asia faces this acutely. Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand are building gigawatts of data center capacity in regions where water availability is already declining due to urbanization and climate change.

I built an interactive water stress calculator that lets you model your facility's water consumption against local basin availability. The results are eye-opening for anyone planning a facility in a water-stressed region.
