The Next Constraint on AI Is Not Chips or Power. It Might Be Water.

58% of global data centers are in water-stressed regions. A single 100MW AI facility drinks 1-2 billion liters per year.

Most operators do not even track water consumption per MW.

I mapped every planned data center in Southeast Asia against regional water stress data. The results:

Jakarta/Bekasi: 85% water stress with 1,050MW+ planned. The most at-risk location in the region.
Johor Bahru: 18% stress — abundant water supply relative to planned capacity.
Da Nang: 18% stress — similar advantage.
Bangkok metro: 42% stress — moderate risk with 230MW planned.

Site selection based on power cost and land availability alone ignores the resource that might run out first.

Water-cooled systems (cooling towers, evaporative cooling) consume 1.8 liters per kWh. Air-cooled systems use near zero. The technology choice matters enormously — but only 23% of new SEA builds are specifying air-cooled chillers.

The WUE (Water Usage Effectiveness) metric exists but is barely tracked. Industry average: 1.8 L/kWh. Best in class: 0.5 L/kWh. The gap represents billions of liters annually across the region.
