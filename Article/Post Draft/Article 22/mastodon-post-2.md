Why can't silicon make lasers?

Silicon has an indirect bandgap — it's terrible at converting electricity to light efficiently. You need III-V semiconductor materials like Indium Phosphide (InP) and Gallium Arsenide (GaAs).

That's why NVIDIA can't just buy TSMC wafers and call it a day. They need Lumentum for CW and UHP lasers, and Coherent for the broader optical networking stack including VCSELs and transceivers.

The silicon photonics revolution requires a marriage of silicon fabrication and III-V laser physics.
