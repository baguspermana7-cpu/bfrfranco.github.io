QUORA ANSWER DRAFT
==================
Target Question: "Should data centers use in-house technicians or outsource maintenance?"
(Also fits: "How to reduce MTTR in critical facilities?", "What is the risk of vendor dependency?", "In-house vs outsourced maintenance pros and cons?")

---

ANSWER:

Here's a scenario I've seen play out at 2 AM more than once:

UPS module fails. Your team calls the vendor. Vendor dispatches from another city. ETA: 6-12 hours. Meanwhile, you're running on reduced redundancy and praying nothing else trips.

Now imagine the same scenario, but your in-house electrical engineer diagnoses the failed IGBT module in 20 minutes, swaps from on-site spares inventory, and restores full redundancy in under 2 hours.

The difference isn't just MTTR. It's decision latency.

When you outsource critical maintenance, you outsource decision-making speed. Every escalation adds 15-45 minutes. Every vendor coordination call adds friction. Every "we need to check with our specialist" extends your exposure window.

But going fully in-house isn't the answer either. The right model is a hybrid with clear boundaries:

- In-house: first response, diagnostics, routine maintenance, emergency procedures, operational decisions
- Vendor: OEM-specific repairs, firmware updates, warranty work, specialized testing equipment

The decision framework I use: if the failure mode has a >2% annual probability AND the MTTR difference between in-house vs vendor response exceeds 4 hours, build the in-house capability.
