59% of Your Downtime Is Just Waiting for a Vendor to Show Up

I analyzed 428 incidents across multiple data center facilities. The biggest contributor to total resolution time was not diagnosis. Not repair. Not root cause analysis.

It was vendor mobilization.

Average breakdown of MTTR:
- Detection to escalation: 22 minutes
- Escalation to vendor dispatch: 1.7 hours
- Vendor travel + site access: 2.45 hours
- Actual diagnosis + repair: 1.8 hours

The vendor contract guarantees 4-hour response. That is response — not resolution. The gap between "a technician is on the way" and "the system is back online" is where downtime costs pile up.

In-house teams cut total MTTR from 6.75 hours to 2.60 hours. A 61% reduction.

"But in-house is more expensive." Is it?

When you factor in: avoided downtime cost per hour, knowledge retention (vendor technicians change, your team doesn't), faster root cause identification (they know YOUR system), and the compounding effect of institutional knowledge...

The break-even point for building in-house capability is lower than most CFOs assume.
