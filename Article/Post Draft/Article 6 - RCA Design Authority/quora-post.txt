Your RCA Process Produces Beautiful Reports That Change Nothing

60% of major data center incidents have a root cause that was already found in a previous investigation.

Read that again.

The same failure mode. Already identified. Already documented. Already "closed out" in the incident management system. And here it is again — because nobody had the authority to change the system that produced it.

This is the design authority gap. Most organizations separate the team that investigates from the team that designs. The investigators write recommendations. The design team evaluates them against competing priorities. Months pass. The recommendation gets deferred. Another incident occurs.

RCA without design authority is organizational theater. You can have the best investigators, the most thorough analysis, and the most accurate root cause identification — and still change nothing.

The fix is not better investigation techniques. The fix is structural: giving the investigation team the mandate and resources to implement system-level changes based on their findings.

I built an interactive Design Authority Scorecard that helps organizations assess whether their RCA process has actual teeth or is just generating documentation.
