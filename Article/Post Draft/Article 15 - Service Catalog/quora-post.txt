I Built a 135-Service Data Center Catalog. The Revenue Rankings Surprised Me.

How do you price data center services across 4 global markets? I decided to find out.

I cataloged 135+ distinct services across 12 categories — colocation, managed services, connectivity, cloud, security, consulting, and more. Then I ranked every single one by Annual Revenue Potential.

The result that surprised me most:

A single 5MW colocation deal generates $7.2M-$13.2M per year in recurring revenue. That one deal outperforms dozens of high-ticket consulting engagements combined.

The services with the highest revenue per engagement are not the most technically complex. They are the ones with:
- Recurring commitments (not project-based)
- Power-based pricing (scales with density)
- Multi-year contracts (sticky revenue)

The consulting and advisory services that most operators invest heavily in? They generate 5-15% of total revenue despite consuming disproportionate senior staff time.

This does not mean consulting is worthless — it is the gateway that converts into colo deals. But the pricing and resource allocation should reflect that funnel reality.

Regional pricing varies dramatically:
- Americas: highest absolute prices, lowest growth rate
- Europe: regulated pricing, highest compliance overhead
- SEA: fastest growth, widest price variance
- Australia: premium pricing, smallest addressable market
