QUORA ANSWER DRAFT
==================
Target Question: "What services do data centers sell?"
(Also fits: "How do colocation data centers make money?", "What is included in data center pricing?", "How much does data center space cost?")

---

ANSWER:

Most people think data centers sell rack space and power. That's like saying restaurants sell tables and chairs.

I cataloged 135+ distinct services across 12 categories that modern data centers offer. The real revenue comes from services most customers don't even know they're paying for.

The 12 categories:

1. Colocation (space, power, cooling — the basics)
2. Connectivity (cross-connects, carrier access, IX peering, dark fiber)
3. Cloud on-ramp (direct connections to AWS, Azure, GCP — this is a $2B+ market)
4. Managed infrastructure (remote hands, hardware lifecycle, monitoring)
5. Security (physical access, CCTV, biometrics, DDoS mitigation)
6. Compliance (SOC 2 audits, PCI DSS, HIPAA environments)
7. Disaster recovery (secondary sites, backup, replication)
8. Network services (SD-WAN, load balancing, DNS)
9. Professional services (design, migration, consulting)
10. Power services (dedicated feeds, generator testing, power conditioning)
11. Environmental monitoring (DCIM dashboards, sensor networks)
12. Value-added (smart hands, asset tracking, cable management)

Pricing varies dramatically by region. A 42U cabinet in Singapore costs $2,000-4,500/month. The same cabinet in Jakarta costs $800-1,800. In Northern Virginia, $1,200-2,500.

The highest-margin services aren't space and power (commodity, 15-25% margin). They're connectivity and managed services (60-80% margin). A single cross-connect generates $200-500/month recurring revenue with near-zero incremental cost.
