==================
Target Question: "Where should data centers be built in Southeast Asia?"
(Also fits: "Why is Southeast Asia attractive for data centers?", "What is the data center market opportunity in SEA?", "Which SEA countries are best for data center investment?")

---

ANSWER:

$37 billion. That's the estimated investment flowing into Southeast Asian data centers through 2030.

But here's what most market reports miss: the opportunity isn't just "build capacity and they will come." It's shaped by three forces that most analysts underweight:

1. Jevons Paradox in action. As cloud and AI become cheaper and more accessible, demand doesn't plateau — it explodes. SEA's digital economy went from $32B in 2015 to $218B in 2023. The infrastructure hasn't kept pace.

2. Sovereign AI mandates. Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and Vietnam all have data localization requirements in various stages. Indonesia's GR 71/2019 requires certain data categories to be processed domestically. This creates captive demand that can't be served from Singapore.

3. Hyperscaler CAPEX explosion. AWS, Google, Microsoft, and Meta are collectively spending $600B+ on infrastructure in 2025-2026. A significant portion targets SEA expansion — Google's $2B Indonesia investment, AWS's $9B Singapore commitment, Microsoft's $2.2B Malaysia push.

The country-by-country picture:

- Singapore: Mature, constrained (moratorium recently lifted with green conditions). Premium pricing. Best connectivity.
- Malaysia (Johor): Massive pipeline, proximity to Singapore, but execution risk on power grid.
- Indonesia: Largest addressable market (280M people), but regulatory complexity and infrastructure gaps.
- Thailand: Emerging hub, strong grid, government incentives.
- Vietnam: Fast-growing digital economy, challenging regulatory environment.
