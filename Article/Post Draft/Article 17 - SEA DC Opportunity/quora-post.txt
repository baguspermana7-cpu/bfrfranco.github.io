The Bear Case for SEA Data Centers Has a Fatal Flaw. It Ignores Jevons Paradox.

DeepSeek cut AI training cost by 95%. Bears said infrastructure demand would collapse.

What actually happened:
- Meta raised capex to $65B
- Amazon to $200B
- Microsoft held at $80B
- Total hyperscaler capex: $600B+

Cheaper AI does not reduce demand. It explodes it. Per-unit cost drops 90%, total usage rises 1,000%. Every efficiency gain the bears cite as evidence AGAINST data center demand is evidence FOR it.

This is Jevons Paradox — first observed with coal in 1865, now replaying with compute in 2026.

But the bull case goes deeper than Jevons:

Sovereign AI mandates: 6 SEA nations now legally require local AI infrastructure. Indonesia targets $140B AI GDP by 2030. Malaysia committed RM 2B for sovereign AI cloud. This is 1,500-2,000 MW of demand backed by law, invisible in commercial pipeline analyses.

Legacy demand: 65% of DC demand through 2030 requires only 3-15 kW/rack. Enterprise IT, cloud, edge, CDN, government. The "stranded" legacy assets serve the majority of actual market demand.

AI inference locality: projected at 90 GW by 2030, must be LOCAL. Every Indonesian AI app needs inference in Indonesia. Every Malaysian chatbot needs a local endpoint.

The bubble narrative miscounts demand by at least 60%.
