Are AI Data Centers Grid Parasites — Or the Biggest Clean Energy Investors in History?

The narrative says data centers drain the grid. Here is what the data says:

Amazon alone contracted 34 GW of renewable energy. That exceeds Malaysia's entire installed grid capacity.

Google has been carbon-neutral since 2007 and is pursuing 24/7 carbon-free energy matching by 2030.

Microsoft committed $10B to carbon removal and is funding next-generation nuclear (small modular reactors) specifically for DC loads.

The E3 study found that data centers generate $33,500 per MW in annual grid surplus value — more revenue than they consume in grid services.

The reason is structural: hyperscalers sign long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs) for renewables that would not get built without guaranteed offtake. They fund grid interconnection upgrades that benefit all users. They invest in battery storage that stabilizes the grid during peak demand.

Without hyperscaler PPAs, much of the global renewable pipeline simply does not get financed. Developers need creditworthy long-term buyers. Hyperscalers are the most creditworthy buyers in the energy market.

This is not corporate greenwashing. This is $100B+ in actual infrastructure investment reshaping how grids get built and funded globally.
