## Post 2 — Why L0-L6
Most commissioning models start at L1 (factory testing) and end at L5 (IST).

But RFS success is often determined before L1 starts — in L0 design planning, where the OPR, Cx plan, points list, and energization strategy are developed.

And handover quality matters too — L6 covers punch list resolution, training, systems manuals, and formal sign-off.

Built a calculator that covers all seven phases.

## Post 3 — Hyperscale & AI
Commissioning a 100MW AI factory campus is fundamentally different from a 2MW enterprise facility.

- DLC adds ~45% cost, ~35% duration
- CDU redundancy and liquid loop zoning testing
- Rack-level thermal acceptance at 50-100+ kW/rack
- Multi-building phased turnover
- Compressed FAT/SAT/IST windows
