# FF-2 Video Script: "The Engineer Shortage Is Fake"
Platform: TikTok / YouTube Shorts / Reels
Target length: 7-9 minutes
Tone: Direct, analytical, slightly provocative — not rage-bait

---

## HOOK (0:00 - 0:08)

[CAMERA: tight framing, direct to lens]

"There are 340,000 unfilled data center jobs right now. And only 1% of employers are willing to train anyone to fill them."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: 340,000 UNFILLED JOBS. 1% WILL TRAIN.]

"So before you believe there's a talent shortage — let's look at who actually created this."

---

## SECTION 1: The Claim (0:08 - 1:00)

[SHOW: news headlines — "Data Center Talent Crisis", "Engineer Shortage Threatens AI Boom"]

"Every major report says the same thing. There aren't enough data center engineers. AI is growing too fast. Critical infrastructure is at risk."

"And some of that is true. The shortage is real in the sense that seats are empty."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: 58% of operators say they're struggling to hire]

"But here's what those headlines leave out."

---

## SECTION 2: The 1% Stat (1:00 - 2:30)

[CAMERA: step closer, lower tone]

"I want you to really hear this number."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: ONLY 1% OF DATA CENTER EMPLOYERS WILL TRAIN ENTRY-LEVEL HIRES]

"One percent. In a global industry facing a 340,000-person gap — 99% of companies have decided that training new engineers is not their problem."

[SHOW: side-by-side comparison — job description requirements vs. years the role has existed]

"At the same time, job descriptions for roles like GPU infrastructure engineer — roles that barely existed before 2022 — are requiring five or more years of experience."

"You cannot require five years of experience for a job that didn't exist five years ago and then complain the talent pool is too shallow."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: JD REQUIRES 5YRS EXPERIENCE. ROLE DIDN'T EXIST 5 YRS AGO.]

"That's not a market problem. That's a writing problem."

---

## SECTION 3: Entry-Level Collapse (2:30 - 3:45)

[SHOW: bar chart — entry-level hiring rates 2018-2024]

"Here's what makes it worse. Since 2020, entry-level hiring in data centers collapsed — down 25 to 60 percent depending on the region."

"The people who should be your mid-level engineers today? They were never hired as juniors."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: ENTRY-LEVEL HIRING DOWN 25-60% SINCE 2020]

"Companies pulled back on junior talent at exactly the moment they should have been building their bench. And now they're surprised the bench is empty."

---

## SECTION 4: The Demographic Bomb (3:45 - 5:00)

[SHOW: age distribution chart — workforce over 60]

"Now layer this on top of the retirement math."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: 32% OF CURRENT WORKFORCE IS OVER 60]

"32% of the existing data center workforce is over 60 years old. These are the people who built and maintained the infrastructure we depend on. And they are leaving."

"Retirements are already accelerating. There is no pipeline to replace them."

[SHOW: timeline graphic — retirements accelerating vs. hiring rate flat]

"An unplanned outage costs over $300,000 per hour. The cost of running a training program or apprenticeship is a fraction of that. The math is not complicated."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: $300K+/HR OUTAGE COST. TRAINING COSTS LESS.]

---

## SECTION 5: The New Roles Nobody Is Training For (5:00 - 6:15)

[SHOW: job listing mockups — GPU infrastructure, liquid cooling]

"And now AI is creating entirely new job categories: GPU infrastructure engineer, liquid cooling systems specialist."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: NEW ROLES PAYING $160K-$230K — PIPELINES EMPTY]

"These roles are paying $160,000 to $230,000 a year. They're critical. And they sit unfilled for months because no one built a training path to get people into them."

"The demand is there. The compensation is there. The infrastructure to develop talent is not."

---

## SECTION 6: Who Is Getting It Right (6:15 - 7:15)

[SHOW: map — Singapore, Germany highlighted]

"Not every country is failing at this. Singapore and Germany both implemented structured workforce development programs — government and industry working together, building specific pipelines for data center roles."

"Both are closing their gaps faster than the US and UK."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: SINGAPORE + GERMANY: STRUCTURED PROGRAMS. NARROWING THE GAP.]

"The difference is not that they have more engineers. It's that they decided to make some."

---

## SECTION 7: What This Means (7:15 - 8:00)

[CAMERA: pull back slightly, reflective tone]

"So is the shortage fake? Not entirely. The seats are genuinely empty. The retirements are real. The AI infrastructure demand is real."

"But the cause? Unrealistic job descriptions. Below-market salaries — 15 to 30 percent below comparable cloud roles. Near-zero training investment. And entry-level hiring that basically stopped."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: MANUFACTURED SHORTAGE. REAL CONSEQUENCES.]

"This is a set of choices that guarantee the gap gets worse. And it doesn't have to be."
