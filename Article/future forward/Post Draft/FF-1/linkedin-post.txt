Traditional websites are dying. But not in the way you think.

I spent weeks researching what actually happened to the open web since 2020. The numbers paint a clear picture: organic Google traffic to independent sites dropped 30-40% in sectors where AI overviews now appear. Meanwhile, platform dependency keeps growing.

Here's what the data actually shows:

The web didn't die overnight. It's been slowly suffocating under three converging forces:

1. AI-generated answers replacing the need to click through
2. Walled-garden platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Reddit) capturing attention that used to flow to websites
3. The economics of content creation making independent publishing increasingly unsustainable

But there's a counter-narrative that most "websites are dead" takes miss entirely. Some categories of websites are thriving MORE than ever. The ones that can't be replicated by AI summaries. The ones offering interactive tools, real-time data, and community-driven content.

The real question isn't whether websites are dying. It's which ones survive -- and what replaces the rest.
