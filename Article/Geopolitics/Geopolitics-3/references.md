## Cable criticality and repair dynamics

- TeleGeography, "Written Congressional Testimony on Subsea Cable Infrastructure" (PDF)
  https://resources.telegeography.com/hubfs/Tim%20Stronge%20Written%20Congressional%20Testimony%20%7C%20TeleGeography.pdf
  Used for the claims that submarine cables carry more than 99% of intercontinental data, support more than $12 trillion in daily financial transactions, remain far more economical than satellites per unit of data, and face repair-start delays of roughly 1.5 months on average.

## Analytical notes

- The latency range used in the article is a modeled inference, not a direct citation. It uses a standard physical approximation for fiber propagation delay plus a congestion premium assumption.
- Country exposure rankings are inferential and based on public topology, operator statements, landing geography, and observed resilience patterns. They should not be interpreted as live operational intelligence.
- The calculator is intentionally a scenario model. It is meant to compare structural exposure, not predict real-time outage behavior.
